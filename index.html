About Prof. Rany M. Salem

Associate Professor, School of Public Health, UC San Diego.
Ph.D. in Statistics (2009), UC San Diego, dissertation: “Statistical methods for genetic association analysis involving complex longitudinal data”.

Specializes in statistical genetics, genetic epidemiology, and analysis of multivariate & longitudinal phenotypes.

Research Areas

  • Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) of metabolic and kidney-related traits
  • Bayesian mixed-models and multivariate longitudinal data methods
  • Statistical genetics related to obesity, diabetic complications, birthweight, and renal disease
